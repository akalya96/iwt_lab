Andy Warhol (1928-1987)
American artist and leading figure in the pop art movement.Known for his works that explore the relationship between artistic expression, culture, and advertisement. Famous works: "Campbell's Soup Cans," "Marilyn Diptych," and "Eight Elvises."
Mark Rothko (1903-1970)
American painter of Latvian Jewish decent,associated with abstract expressionism. Known for his color field paintings, characterized by large, soft-edged rectangles of color. Famous works: "No. 61 (Rust and Blue)," "Orange, Red, Yellow," and "Untitled (Black on Grey)."
Jean-Micheal Basquiat (1960-1988)
American artist who emerged from New York City graffiti scene.Known for his raw, expressive works that combined text and imagery. Famous works: "Untitled (1981)," "Hollywood Africans," and "Horn Players."
Frida Kahlo (1907-1954)
Mexican painter known for her self-portraits and works inspired by nature and Mexican culture.Her art is characterized by its vibrant colors and surrealist elements. Famous works: "The Two Fridas," "Self-Portrait with Thorn Necklace and Hummingbird," and "The Broken Column."
Roy Lichtendtein (1923-1997)
American pop artist known for his comic strip-style works.Utilized a technique that mimicked the Ben-Day dots printing method used in comic books. Famous works: "Whaam!," "Drowning Girl," and "Look Mickey."
Geogia o'Keeffe (1887-1986)
American pop artist known for her enlarged flowers,New York skyscrapers,and New Mexico landscapes.Her work is characterized by its precision and clarity. Famous works: "Jimson Weed/White Flower No. 1," "Black Iris III," and "Red Canna."
